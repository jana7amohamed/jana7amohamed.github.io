About Me

I'm Jana Abd-Elkader, a girl with a unique outlook on the world, experiencing it with vibrant and distinct vibes. Though my perspective may not resonate with everyone, it often inspires admiration, leaving many to exclaim, "WOW". I enjoy reciting poetry, bringing the words of others to life with my voice. Every piece in this portfolio is a reflection of my passion for poetry.

Abou-Feras Al-Hamadany

The provided lines express profound longing and melancholy, contrasting the sweetness of love with the bitterness of life. The speaker wishes for the beloved's approval and a flourishing connection between them, feeling isolated from the world. Love is depicted as transformative, making all struggles seem insignificant. The poet uses vivid imagery and metaphors, comparing love to pure water and emphasizing the unattainability of desires through the imagery of a mirage. Overall, the verses reflect a deep emotional yearning for connection, highlighting the complexities and challenges of love.

Mohamed Al-Gharabawy: سائلوا بارليف

The provided lines celebrate the resilience and strength of the people of Sinai in the face of oppression. The speaker reflects on the powerful echo of their cries, emphasizing the suffering endured under tyranny. The moment when they proclaim "God is the Greatest" is pivotal, shaking the foundations of falsehood and instilling fear in the oppressors. The lines convey a sense of victory as the stronghold of the oppressors collapses, ending the myth of their invincibility. Ultimately, the poem highlights the triumph of justice over tyranny, illustrating the indomitable spirit of those who resist and fight for their freedom.

Tamim El-Barghothy: في القدس

The lines express the profound and miraculous nature of Jerusalem, where contradictions coexist and wonders are undeniable. The imagery of flipping through pieces of fabric symbolizes the city's rich history, blending the old with the new. The miracles in Jerusalem are tangible and can be felt through personal interactions, whether greeting an elder or touching a building. The poet suggests that these experiences leave an indelible mark, like an inscription of a poem on one's hands, connecting the visitor to the heritage and honor of the city. Overall, the verses celebrate the deep historical and spiritual significance of Jerusalem, inviting a sense of reverence and connection.
